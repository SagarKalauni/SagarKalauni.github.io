Duties

  • My master’s thesis was on “Handling Rare Event Data with Finite Sample Correction and SMOTE.” In this work, we modified the logistic regression model to improve its predictive ability in the context of rare event data. As an example, we used this corrected model to predict breast cancer in patients, which is a one of the cases of rare event data. We didn’t stop there—we also applied a data-based technique (SMOTE) to handle rare event data. Finally, we demonstrated how predictions improved significantly after applying the model correction as well as the data-based technique. To showcase the effectiveness of our corrected model, we performed simulation studies. We simulated millions of data points with rare event scenarios, applied our corrected model, and showed that its coefficients provided more accurate estimates of the actual population values. This led to more reliable predictions. Our study particularly highlighted how well the model performed when the sample size was small. For all simulations, I used R programming.
